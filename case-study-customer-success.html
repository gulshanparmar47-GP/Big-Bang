The Problem

"Customer Success" is often confused with "customer support."

New CS hires frequently start the role thinking their job is reactive — answer tickets, fix issues, keep people happy. This module reframes Customer Success as a proactive discipline: reading signals early, checking in before being asked, and measuring success by whether the customer actually achieved their goal — not just whether a case was closed. The learning objective: by the end of the module, a learner should be able to distinguish proactive CS judgment calls (health scoring, QBRs, triage) from reactive support instincts, and apply that judgment to realistic decision points.

Design Rationale

Why a conversation, not a narrator

01

Peer framing over authority framing

A narrator stating a definition reads as being told something. Two colleagues arriving at the same insight through conversation reads as discovering it — a small shift that changes how much a learner actually internalizes a definition versus just hearing it.

02

Learner-paced reveal

Click-to-advance (rather than autoplay) puts pacing control in the learner's hands, appropriate for a definitional/conceptual screen where rushing past the core idea would undercut the rest of the module.

03

The payoff line lands last

The actual definition — "the discipline of making sure customers reach their goals" — is deliberately the final line, not the first. The conversation builds toward it rather than opening with it, mirroring how the concept should land for a new hire encountering it for the real first time.
