Why Instructional Design

My path here isn't traditional. That's the point.

I currently work in Stakeholder Experience at Apeejay Education Society — designing and analyzing surveys across parents, teachers, and students. That role turned out to be closer to instructional design than it looks on paper.

In my current role

Survey design across three audiences

Writing instruments for New Parent Admissions, Class Teacher, and Higher-Ed Student surveys — each requiring a different tone, reading level, and set of assumptions about the respondent.

In my current role

Data analysis to guide iteration

Reading survey results back into decisions — what question wasn't understood, what needs rewording, what the data actually implies about the audience.

translates directly into ↓
In instructional design

Audience analysis

The ADDIE "Analyze" phase — understanding who the learner is, what they already believe, and what will make them tune out — is the same discipline as writing a survey that gets honest answers.

In instructional design

Evidence-based iteration

Good ID isn't written once — it's revised against how real learners respond, the same way a survey instrument gets revised against real respondent data.
